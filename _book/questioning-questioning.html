14 Questioning {questioning}

14.1 How much experience and how many personal projects did you have prior to applying?

Employers won’t expect you to have lots of experience`` orprojects`` at this stage, but any that you do have will help your application stand out. It doesn’t have to be paid experience: Volunteering, getting involved in competitions as a competitor or organiser will help see chapter 5

14.2 What is the application process like for the placement program? When you find a company/program that you have been accepted onto, how do you register it with the uni?

See wiki

14.3 How often did you get rejected?

Rejection is a normal part of job hunting, you need to learn to live with it and not take it personally (ref). You only need one job offer

14.4 When should we start applying for placement or internship?

The best time to start applying is September, and the time of year you apply will determine what kind of opportunities are availalbe, see (ref). Big employers tend to close their applications anytime between September and December. If you’ve not found anything you like by January, you can change your job search strategy (ref)

14.5 How many placement students receive a return offer for after graduating?

In the UK, around 50% of students go back to their placement

We don’t currently have good data on how many students get return offers, but a placement (or internship) is a bit like a long job interview. If they like you, it is likely you’ll receive an offer to return when you graduate.

  • Is it possible to land a placement without any prior experience?

Yes, see @ref{qexp}

14.6 Average pay

Pay varies by sector, location and role, see (ref)

14.7 How much help do you get from the university when applying?

Your University can help you but ultimately finding a job is your responsbility, not the University see (ref). Talk to your peers, make use of PASS, network (ref) go to careers fairs, put yourself out there, see Jonathan’s story 28.

Speak to your employability tutor and careers service. If you’re a Manchester student, keep an eye on the Wednesday Waggle.

  • What is the pay for the meta internship?

Google it

14.8 Did anyone get an internship outside of the UK? What was the process like?

Did they pay for accommodation/flights ect. or was it up to you?

It is possible to do a placement outside the UK, but it takes more organisation and there are more checks the University has to do to approve your placement.

14.9 Did you find that majority of IE students got a placement?

If you’re serious about finding a placement, you will be able to get one. However, it isn’t easy.

  • Running out of time
  • Underestimating what is involved
  • Not doing enough applications (optimising quality at the expense of quantity)
  • Doing too many applications (optimising quantity at the expense of quality)

14.10 How much responsibility / real work do you get?

Varies with employer. This is a good question to ask in interview e.g. ``Do you have any questions for us’’. Some employers give students internal projects never see the light of day.

Other employers will throw you in at the deep-end.

If you’d like more responsibility you should ask for it, but you might have to prove yourself first and earn the trust and respect of your manager.

14.11 How to find opportunities at startup companies?

Startups are harder to find because you are less likely to have heard of them. Go to events, see e.g. (ref)

14.12 When you have been accepted onto the program and you have signed the contract, what do you have to do to arrange things with the uni? Is there anything you need to do on my placement?

see (ref)

14.13 For those who worked at a big firm or startups, do you feel as if they had a very well structured training program?

One of the advantages of working for bigger organisation is they will often have established training programs you can take part of.

Startups tend to be a bit more seat of your pants style training, which can also be valuable.

Whever you work, you should always ask for training

14.14 How did you prepare for coding interviews {#qcode}?

See (ref)

14.15 What kind of projects/portfolio/skills do I need to secure an AI/ ML industrial placement?

Open source, see e.g. hackathons, will all help.

14.16 How did you sort out accommodation? Was it stressful? When did you sort it?

If you’re lucky, your employers may help you find accommodation, they might even be able to put you in touch with other students employed by them.

But like finding the job, the accommodation is your responsbility to sort out, which can be stressful. E.g. finding a place to live in London where it is more expensive than Manchester.

#3 How to negotiate payment if it is not shown on the employers website?

Some employers drive a hard bargain and look to pay you the minimum they can get away with.

So, know your value (ref) and prepare to negotiate. Learn how to negotiate better (ref)

14.17 If you get an offer early on, should you hold out for a better company? How long do you have to accept it?

It’s complicated, see (ref)

14.18 How to prepare for Video Interview? What are must-do and what are things that we cannot do

You can rehearse standard interview questions, see shortlistme

14.19 Do we need to prepare for all commonly asked questions?

Any preparation you do is likely to

  • Make you less nervous
  • Help you answer questions better

14.20 What makes an Intership or placement stand out on a CV or in an interview {q}

Any internship or placement that you’ve done before you graduate will make your application stand out when you’re applying for graduate jobs or graduate schemes when you finish your degree, see (ref)

14.21 What was the most useful thing to have during the application proces? (I.e experience to talk about or leetcode practice)

That’s going to depend on where you’re applying

Not all employers use coding interviews Small companies tend to have much quicker and simpler recruitment e.g. one interview rather than four (say)

14.22 Where do you look for the majority of the roles to you apply for

See chapter 11, and particularly (ref) gradjobs

14.23 Did having a linkden help?

It can help to have a digital profile on linkedin or github, but it isn’t a requirement.

  • For the big competitive companies like Google, Meta…etc. what sort of experience do I need?

See chapter 5

End of August

14.27 Is it difficult getting back into student mode? (

Many students return from placement to do a stronger final year because they can apply the skills and experience acquired that can help e.g.

  • Personal and social skills like time management
  • Technical knowledge that can be applied in a final year project for example

14.28 If you moved away for your placement, what did you do about social stuff since you didnt have uni life and events? Is it not a bit isolating?

It can be, you have to start all over again like a fresher. When you return, some of your fellow students will have graduated, but here are lots of students out on placement, so you’re not on your own

14.29 Did you write a cover letter?

Its worth writing a covering letter, even if nobody reads it, because if nothing else, it gets your elevator pitch right. The three questions your answer in a covering letter are all likely to come up in an interview anyway, so having thought about and articulated your answers means you’re more likely to come across well in an interview.

Some employers read covering letters closely, see (ref)

14.30 When does the placement year start?

Anytime between June and September. Minimum is 9 months, most are 12 months, some are 15 (extended)

14.31 How do you learn how to program? I feel I know nothing

ok

By the end of second year, you know the fundamentals. You’re not an expert, but you know enough to start your career. Computing is a professional wher e you are constantly learning. Stay in school (ref)

14.32 Should you wait until you have more things to put on your CV to apply or should you just apply as early as possible

This is a balancing act, applications take time that could otherwise be spent bolstering your CV.

If you don’t apply, you won’t be invited to interview.

It can help, but employers shouldn’t require it.

If you don’t mind having information about yourself in the public domain, linkedin is a good way to talk about projects, experience and certifications that you can’t fit on a one or two page CV, see (ref)

14.42 How much do first year marks effect applications?

First year marks are a factor, especially for more competitive opportunities.

If you’ve not performed as well as you’d like in first year, you might choose to distinguish between your achieved and expected degree classification

14.43 Do you need to satisfy all the requirements mentioned in a job advert?

Its rare for a candidate to satisy everything on job spec, see (ref)

14.44 Which cities should we be looking at for placements?

Big cities have more opportunities. Manchester has plenty see git.io/manc, London has even more.

The wider you cast your net (geographically speaking) the more opportunities you’ll find

14.45 What percentage of students recieve placements through the uni rather than applying themselves?

There will be a limited number of internships and placements at your University. E.g in Manchester.

  • Imago
  • ResearchIT
  • Kilburn internships (advertised around May)

14.46 Will all of these vacancies be shown on career connect or do we have to look outside of it?

Career Connect is good, but you should look elsewhere too, see 11 (ref). Each website has advantages and disdvantages, so its not a good idea to just look in one place.

For Manchester students see the pathways

14.48 How do you showcase your passion for technology when you don’t have much coding experience

Personal projects, hackathons (ref), open source, doing courses in your spare time (coursera etc) all showcase your interest.

Your time is finite resource, but anything technical you manage to do over and above the basic requirements of your degree will help your application stand out..

But don’t burn out (ref)
